# GLYPH — Discovery Sequence Analysis

**Author:** Technical Director analysis  
**Audience:** Greg (CEO), Executive Producer  
**Status:** ANALYSIS ONLY — **no implementation authorized**

**Trigger:** CEO Phase B playtest — intentionally disconnected runes to understand "cold" without instruction. *The game naturally creates curiosity.*

**Constitution anchor:** Agency · trust · hope · player intelligence. Onboarding must reinforce, not weaken.

**Frozen:** Mechanics · rules · emotional contract · no redesign in this document.

---

## Executive summary

**Recommendation: Discovery Sequence** — not a traditional tutorial, not a labeled playable tutorial, not zero onboarding.

GLYPH should teach through **authored first experiences** where the board, hand, and aftermath do the work. The CEO's disconnected-rune experiment is the design north star: **curiosity is the product feature; instruction is the risk.**

A Discovery Sequence is not "no teaching." It is **curated teaching without explanation** — the player feels like they figured something out, because they did.

---

## SECTION 1 — Tutorial, playable tutorial, discovery sequence, or none?

### Options evaluated

| Approach | What it is | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **Traditional tutorial** | Modals, arrows, rule text, forced steps | **Reject** — kills curiosity CEO identified; violates calm (Creative Bible); treats player as confused, not intelligent |
| **Playable tutorial** | Guided level with explicit "learn fire" labeling | **Reject for GLYPH** — still frames player as student; "tutorial voice" breaks Meridian whisper |
| **Discovery sequence** | Handcrafted early boards + minimal shell copy; lessons emerge from play | **Recommend** |
| **No onboarding at all** | Random hand, empty board, zero framing | **Reject** — abdicates studio responsibility; relies on luck; anxiety returns ("am I playing right?") |

### Recommendation: **Discovery Sequence**

**Why not traditional or playable tutorial**

1. **CEO evidence:** Experimentation emerged *without* instruction. Adding explanation would optimize away the behavior we want to protect.
2. **Constitution §5:** Performance to author, not lesson to pass. Tutorials imply one correct path.
3. **Constitution §6:** Hope from agency — discovery preserves "I figured this out."
4. **Constitution §7:** Board is evidence. Teaching belongs on the board, not in overlays.
5. **Prior EP/CEO directive:** No tutorials, arrows, dialogue, hint popups — Discovery Sequence complies; traditional tutorial does not.
6. **Creative positioning:** Tetris Effect, Witness, Dorfromantik — teach through doing in a calm room.

**Why not zero onboarding**

Random first hands fail the Vertical Slice question. Some players get a chainable hand and learn nothing about causality. Others get chaos and ask "am I broken?" The studio must **author the first five minutes**, not **lecture** during them.

**What Discovery Sequence includes (minimal, already aligned with slice plan)**

- ≤3 lines total shell copy before first Spark (invitation, not rules)
- **Five handcrafted opening boards** (see Section 2)
- Board-as-mirror aftermath (lit/cold, spark origin — when readable)
- Voluntary replay with no unlock walls

**What it excludes**

- Rule encyclopedias · skill trees in minute one · "correct solution" framing · tutorial UI chrome

## SECTION 2 — The first five boards (handcrafted)

**Design rule:** Each board teaches **one** lesson. Player never sees "Lesson 2 of 5." They see another board, another hand, another chance to author.

**Design rule:** Every board must be **sparkable in under 90 seconds** and produce a **legible aftermath** — even on failure.

**Design rule:** Boards 1–3 allow **safe failure** (no shame copy). Board 4 introduces **branching hope**. Board 5 is **first true authorship test**.

---

### Board 1 — *Connection*

**Quiet lesson:** Runes must **touch** to chain. Disconnected clusters stay cold.

| Design intent | |
|---|---|
| **Hand** | Two identical runes (e.g. two Fire) + filler — enough to place a pair and one orphan |
| **Board** | Empty 5×5 — player chooses layout |
| **Suggested arc** | Player places pair touching, one alone → sparks pair → sees lit vs cold |
| **CEO behavior replicated** | Disconnected rune **by design** — player who experiments with isolation learns the vocabulary of the board |

**Success feeling:** *"Oh — the lonely one didn't fire."*  
**Not:** *"Tutorial complete: adjacency rules."*

---

### Board 2 — *Spark origin*

**Quiet lesson:** **Where** you call Action changes the story. Same stones, different director's choice.

| Design intent | |
|---|---|
| **Hand** | Fixed three-rune line setup potential (e.g. F–W–F or F–F–L) |
| **Board** | **Pre-seeded** three runes in a line (touching) — player adds 0–1 from hand OR places hand to extend |
| **Arc** | Player sparks left end vs right end → different lit sets on aftermath mirror |

**Success feeling:** *"I should have sparked from the other end."*  
**Teaches:** Spark origin (Phase 2 core decision #4) without naming it.

---

### Board 3 — *Reach*

**Quiet lesson:** Lightning **jumps a gap**. Fire does not.

| Design intent | |
|---|---|
| **Hand** | Guaranteed Lightning + Fire + one connector |
| **Board** | Two clusters with **one empty cell** between — Fire cluster on one side, target rune across gap |
| **Arc** | Player tries Fire spark first (one-gap cold visible) → replay or next board with Lightning bridge |

**Alternative within one board:** Hand allows both; player experiments. Aftermath shows **why** gap mattered (cold taxonomy: dotted one-gap).

**Success feeling:** *"It jumped!"* — surprise with hindsight explainability (Phase 2 design line).

**Element scope:** Lightning only — do not introduce Water/Crystal combos yet.

---

### Board 4 — *Branch*

**Quiet lesson:** Chains **split and multiply**. The frontier has more than one direction.

| Design intent | |
|---|---|
| **Hand** | Crystal + Fire support — enough to form a T or Y topology |
| **Board** | Empty or one seed rune — player builds toward a fork |
| **Arc** | First cascade where chain count ≥4 with visible fork; hope HUD earns "Come on…" |

**Success feeling:** *"It went both ways."*  
**Teaches:** Crystal / 8-spread / multiplier **felt**, not read.

---

### Board 5 — *Author*

**Quiet lesson:** You have **enough** to solve this yourself. No new mechanic.

| Design intent | |
|---|---|
| **Hand** | Mixed four-element hand — first time all four appear together |
| **Board** | Empty — **no pre-seed** |
| **Objective** | Beat target — first board where score target is meaningful |
| **Arc** | Player combines connection + origin + reach + branch lessons in one authorship act |

**Success feeling:** *"I built that."* — Vertical Slice emotional contract.  
**Failure still OK:** Aftermath mirror answers *why*; voluntary replay.

---

### Sequence arc (player should feel)

```
Board 1: "Something about touching."
Board 2: "Where I spark matters."
Board 3: "That one reaches farther."
Board 4: "It can split."
Board 5: "I know enough to try."
```

**Never:** *"Welcome to Tutorial Level 3."*

## SECTION 3 — Reference games: teaching without explaining

### Portal

**Method:** Environmental constraint + immediate consequence + safe failure. First chamber impossible to die; puzzle teaches by refusal.

**GLYPH lesson:** First boards must be **low punishment**. Wrong spark shows truth on board, not game over. "The chamber won't open" = "cold runes stay cold."

### The Witness

**Method:** No text; puzzles teach one rule each; island order is semi-free but early puzzles are simpler. Player intelligence is assumed.

**GLYPH lesson:** One rule per board. **No text** doesn't mean no authorship — Witness hand-crafts discovery order. Our first five boards are Witness-style **rule cells**, not an open island yet.

### Super Mario Bros. 1-1

**Method:** First screen teaches jump, pipe, enemy — through geometry and risk. Player can experiment; Goomba is optional lesson.

**GLYPH lesson:** Board 1 must allow **optional experiments** (CEO disconnected rune). Mandatory path teaches connection; optional path rewards curiosity without blocking progress.

### Into the Breach

**Method:** Perfect information; every outcome traceable; small grid; failure shows exactly what happened.

**GLYPH lesson:** Aftermath mirror is our ITB log. Discovery Sequence boards must produce **readable** lit/cold outcomes — or curiosity becomes confusion (CEO P0 feedback: taxonomy too subtle).

### Cross-cutting lessons for GLYPH

| Lesson from references | GLYPH application |
|---|---|
| **One idea per beat** | One lesson per board |
| **Safe to fail** | No eject, no timer, Clear Board always available |
| **Consequence is honest** | §1 never lie — board shows truth |
| **Player experiments rewarded** | Disconnected runes are valid science |
| **Authored early, wide later** | Five handcrafted → then random hands |
| **Assume intelligence** | Whisper copy only |

## SECTION 4 — Preserving curiosity

### The CEO moment (protect at all costs)

> *"I intentionally left runes disconnected to see what would happen."*

That is **scientific play** — hypothesis, experiment, evidence. It is Constitution-perfect.

### How to encourage experimentation without losing players

| Principle | Implementation direction (when authorized) |
|---|---|
| **Low stakes early** | Boards 1–3: target visible but soft — beat optional or very achievable |
| **No wrong experiment** | Disconnected placement never triggers error state |
| **Board answers "what happened?"** | Aftermath mirror (when readable) — not "you failed" |
| **Pick-up and retry** | Right-click / pick-up before Spark — already in build |
| **Curiosity-safe copy** | Never "Invalid placement" · never "Try again!" popup |
| **Hidden rubric delayed** | Target matters most on Board 5 — earlier boards prioritize *understanding* over *grading* |
| **One new variable per board** | Don't introduce Lightning + Crystal + target pressure simultaneously |

### The balance: curious vs lost

| Curious (want) | Lost (avoid) |
|---|---|
| "What if I disconnect one?" | "What am I supposed to do?" |
| "What if I spark here?" | "Is this broken?" |
| "Why did that stay cold?" | "Am I playing correctly?" |
| Voluntary replay | One run and quit |

**Bridge:** Lost becomes curious when **aftermath is readable**. Lost stays lost when player can't distinguish **experiment result** from **mistake**.

CEO P0 evidence: if lit/cold and taxonomy aren't readable, curiosity becomes confusion. **Discovery Sequence depends on board mirror legibility** — not on more words.

## SECTION 5 — What should NEVER be taught (and what should)

### Teaching tiers

| Tier | Mechanics / concepts | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Immediately obvious** | Tap hand → place · tap placed rune → Spark · lit = fired · dim = didn't · you can pick runes back up | Motor loop + outcome binary — friction here kills Minute 1 |
| **Gradually discovered (Boards 1–5)** | Adjacency · spark origin · Lightning gap · branching · combining elements | Core authored discovery arc |
| **Gradually discovered (runs 6–20)** | Steam conversion · multiplier growth · Crystal spike · target strategy · "when to Spark" greed | Emerges from random hands + mirror |
| **Mastered over time (never tutorialized)** | Full combinatorial optimization · speed placement · deep Crystal meta · perfect target routing | Mastery fantasy — player owns the journey |
| **Never taught explicitly** | Optimal solution exists · "correct" board · hidden dev vocabulary ("cold") · multiplier formula · internal BFS order | Preserves performance frame, hope, and discovery |

### NEVER teach (explicit list)

- There is one correct layout per hand
- Element letter codes (F/L/W/C) as homework — shapes/colors carry identity when art arrives
- Multiplier math and scoring formula
- "You should have placed here" (post-hoc arrows)
- Meta progression teases during discovery sequence
- That disconnected runes were a "mistake" — CEO proved they're a **valid experiment**

### SHOULD remain discoveries (even after 100 hours)

- Specific combo chains authored in the moment
- The exact score before Spark (Constitution §12 — numbers confirm feeling)
- How far a hand *could* have gone with perfect play
- Crystal balance at full meta depth (Phase 4 debt — not slice)

## SECTION 6 — How we know onboarding succeeded

### Success is NOT

- Player recites all four element rules
- Player beats target on Board 1
- Player completes onboarding checklist
- Player says "I understand"

### Success IS

- Player is **curious enough to keep experimenting**

### Observable behaviors (Protocol v1 compatible)

| Behavior | What it indicates |
|---|---|
| **Voluntary second Spark** within 10 min | Loop landed — VS-2 signal |
| **Voluntary disconnected placement** (witnessed or self-reported) | Curiosity preserved |
| **Changes spark origin or layout** between attempts without prompt | Authorship, not compliance |
| **Post-run language:** *"because it wasn't touching"* / *"wrong end"* / *"the gap"* | Causality discovered |
| **Absence of:** *"Am I playing right?"* / *"What do I do?"* | Normative anxiety reduced |
| **Presence of hope language** during Board 4–5 cascades | Hope Contract intact |
| **Pick-up and rearrange** before Spark | Safe experimentation |
| **Does not ask facilitator** what cold/lit means after Board 1 | Board taught |

### Failure signals

- Facilitator must explain adjacency after Board 1
- Player asks if disconnected runes are allowed
- One board and exit
- Player treats it as puzzle with one solution ("what's the answer?")

### CEO-specific acceptance test

> Would Greg **intentionally disconnect a rune again** on Board 2 — not because he's confused, but because he's **testing a new hypothesis**?

If yes — Discovery Sequence preserved curiosity while adding structure.

## FINAL QUESTION — Handcrafted forever or procedural?

### Recommendation: **Split by horizon**

| Scope | Handcrafted or procedural | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **First five boards (Discovery Sequence)** | **Handcrafted forever** | These are the "1-1" of GLYPH — authored rule beats, fixed order, QA-able, evidence-comparable across playtests. Procedural cannot guarantee "one lesson per board." |
| **Board 6+ in first session** | **Procedural hands** on empty or lightly varied boards | Variety, replay, hope — random hands already in sim |
| **Full game runs (post-slice)** | **Procedural** with constraint variety (Phase 7) | Content pipeline scales; discovery arc is not repeated every session |

### Why first five should stay fixed

1. **Discovery is authored content** — random generation is the wrong tool for pedagogy.
2. **Evidence discipline** — one-variable playtests require identical opening beats.
3. **Constitutional** — we protect the **first experience**, not every experience.
4. **CEO moment** — Board 1 can **invite** disconnection by hand design; RNG cannot reliably.
5. **Industry precedent** — Mario 1-1, Portal chamber 00, Witness starting area — fixed forever, game still has infinite replay later.

### Why procedural eventually

1. Mastery requires **novel situations** — repeating Board 5 forever becomes tutorial loop.
2. Roguelike identity (pre-production) assumes fresh hands.
3. Horizontal progression (Constitution §11) adds breadth after player graduates discovery arc.

### Graduation model

```
Boards 1–5 (handcrafted, once per player) →
Random hands + targets (indefinite) →
Full run structure (post-slice product)
```

**First five boards are the opening movement, not the whole symphony.**

Player plays them **once per profile** (or once per major version if mechanics change — constitutional amendment territory).
