# GLYPH — Studio Constitution

*Ratified at the close of pre-production. This document is the supreme authority of the GLYPH project. It outlives every mechanic, feature, system, and sprint.*

---

## Preamble

Pre-production is complete — not because every question has been answered, but because **the remaining questions can no longer be answered with documents.** They require implementation.

From this point forward, our mission changes:

> **We are no longer discovering GLYPH.**
> **We are protecting GLYPH.**

We are no longer inventing the game. We are **protecting the emotional contract** we have already chosen.

---

## Authority

The Constitution is **above**:

- Features
- Systems
- Convenience
- Scope
- Production speed

If implementation reveals flaws: **change the implementation.**

**Do not casually change the Constitution.**

If the Constitution must change: treat it like a **constitutional amendment** (see Article IV).

---

## Article I — The Twelve Principles

Every future decision must survive this wall. If it violates any article, it does not ship.

### Trust

**§1. The game never lies.**
No fake suspense, fake uncertainty, or hidden manipulation. Staging amplifies truth; it never fabricates it.

**§8. Trust is sacred.**
If the player caused it, they must know they caused it. Causality is never obscured by RNG, pay-to-win, or opaque rules.

### Hope

**§2. Make players hope.**
The design objective. Evaluate every decision: *does this increase or decrease hope?*

**§3. The explosion is punctuation — not the sentence.**
The sentence is hope — rooting for the frontier. Spectacle serves the sentence; it never replaces it.

**§4. The frontier is the lead actor.**
The player's eye follows the edge of propagation. Not the score box. Not particles. The wave.

**§6. Hope comes from agency — not from randomness at Spark.**
Hope during the performance is hope in one's own decisions. Randomness may vary the stage between performances; it may never decide the performance.

**§10. Grin before optimize.**
Delight precedes depth. If the first response is min-maxing instead of *"come on… one more…,"* we failed.

**§12. If score were hidden, it should still matter.**
Numbers confirm what the player already felt. They do not create the feeling.

### Performance

**§5. The board is a stage. The player is the director.**
Not a puzzle to solve — a performance to author. Spark is *"Action!"*

**§7. Every cascade tells a story. The board is evidence.**
After Spark, the player can reconstruct what happened without a replay log. Fired vs. cold. Path taken.

**§9. The curtain call before the credits.**
Performance first. Score, targets, and meta feedback after the cascade lands — never competing with the frontier during the show.

### Progression & Craft

**§11. The player progresses — not the power bar.**
Progression adds breadth and challenge, never permanent strength that makes outcomes untrustworthy.

**§13. Prototype before doctrine.**
The Constitution protects **proven** truths, not untested assumptions. If repeated player evidence contradicts a principle, initiate a formal amendment — do not ignore evidence, and do not change the Constitution for convenience. *(Amendment XIII — Greg / Creative Director, production open.)*

*(Supporting craft standards from pre-production — not numbered on the wall but binding in review: legibility is a feature; predict smart / delight surprised via cognitive limits; cold runes are hope for the next performance; depth is data not animation.)*

## Article II — The Design Review Question

**Every design review begins with two questions:**

> **1. Which constitutional principle does this feature strengthen?**
> **2. What player evidence justifies building it now?**

If the answer to (1) is **"none"** — it probably doesn't belong.
If the answer to (2) is weak — it waits.

If the answer is **"convenience only"** — it does not belong.

If the answer is **"metrics / retention industry standard"** — verify it does not violate §1, §6, §8, or §11 before proceeding.

---

## Article III — Disciplined Execution

Pre-production produced:

- A validated core loop (Placement → Spark → Cascade → Score)
- A four-element foundation (Fire, Lightning, Water, Crystal)
- Skill, strategy, decision, progression, and constraint architectures (Phases 1–7)
- A red-team go/no-go (prototype-first)
- A passed Spark Test (mechanic survives; spectacle/hope does not yet)
- Choreography, suspense/hope, and studio culture documents

**Phase: Production — protected by this Constitution.**

We no longer write design philosophy documents unless (a) a new foundational truth is discovered, or (b) a constitutional amendment is required. Time belongs to **build → test → observe → refine.**

Execution order (binding until amended by evidence):

1. **Milestone 1: Hope Pass (Spark Test v2)** — same rules; score hidden by default; frontier as lead actor; natural approach only; no fake beats. *Disprove or prove hope.*
2. **Only if hope passes** — expand within constitutional bounds (legibility, story, curtain call).
3. **Never skip the hope gate** for features, content, meta, monetization, or polish.

Speed is subordinate to the emotional contract.

---

## Article IV — Constitutional Amendment

The Constitution changes **only** when evidence proves a principle is wrong — not when implementation is hard, not when a feature is inconvenient, not when a deadline pressures compromise.

**Amendment requires documentation of:**

1. **Why the principle failed** — which article, under what conditions.
2. **What evidence forced the change** — playtest data, tester quotes, measurable failure (not opinion, not preference, not "industry norm").
3. **What emotional consequence follows** — what the player will feel differently; what trust or hope is gained or lost.

**Amendment does not require unanimity — but it requires honesty.**

Casual exceptions are forbidden. "Just this once" is how constitutions die.
