# GLYPH — Meridian Room DNA & Product / World Framework

**Status:** Approved by Executive Producer.  
**Role clarification:** The Meridian Room is **environmental DNA** — not the product, not the marketing hook, not the player-facing fantasy.

---

## What The Meridian Room is

The Meridian Room is the **internal reference space** that quietly informs:

| Influenced by DNA | How (invisible to player) |
|---|---|
| **Architecture** | Circular containment, single focal surface, generous negative space |
| **Materials** | Slate, stone, brass, crystal — honest, aged, tactile |
| **Lighting** | Dim room, local glow, blue-hour band, points not floods |
| **Audio** | Room tone, breath between events, soft ticks not stingers |
| **Pacing** | Enter · arrange · pause · initiate · watch · hold · exhale |

The player **never needs to know** the room exists as a concept.

They should leave saying:

> *"This place made me feel calm."*

Not:

> *"I learned about observatories."*

---

## What The Meridian Room is not

| Not this | Why |
|---|---|
| **The product** | The product is a **universally satisfying strategy game** — placement, spark, cascade, mastery |
| **The marketing hook** | Hook is **hope + calm intelligence**, not "play an observatory simulator" |
| **Player-facing fantasy** | No lore requirement, no setting exposition, no "you are an astronomer" |
| **Visible branding** | No meridian lines as UI motif unless they **serve clarity** |
| **Narrative wrapper** | World supports emotion; **never replaces** the loop |

**The world should support the emotion. Never replace it.**

**First responsibility (unchanged):** A universally satisfying strategy game.  
**Meridian Room exists to reinforce** the emotional contract — comfort, calm, hope, mastery — **not redefine it.**

---

## The disappearance test

Every world-facing choice must pass:

> **If we removed all explicit "observatory" signaling, would the player still feel the same calm?**

- **Yes** → DNA is working (world disappeared into experience)
- **No** → we built **set dressing** instead of **emotion** — pull back

The world **disappears into the experience**; it does not compete with it.

---

## Product vs. World — decision framework

**Separate every design decision into one bucket before shipping it.**

### PRODUCT

**Would increase installs, retention, comprehension, or satisfaction of the core loop** — for any player, including one who ignores all atmosphere.

Examples:
- Frontier visible during cascade (hope)
- Legible cause-and-effect (trust)
- Score after curtain call (Constitution §9)
- Clear rune rules on first session
- One-thumb placement on mobile
- Deterministic resolution
- Retry without punishment
- Fair difficulty curve

**Product question:** *Does this make the strategy game clearer, fairer, or more satisfying?*

---

### WORLD

**Would increase immersion, calm, or emotional place** — without changing whether the game works.

Examples:
- Slate texture on cells vs flat grey
- Blue-hour gradient in background
- Soft brass edge on rune frames
- Ventilation room tone in empty menu
- Slower ambient gradient drift
- Meridian-adjacent **material** language (not literal meridian UI)

**World question:** *Does this make the player feel calmer or more "inside" the experience — without teaching them lore?*

---

## The rule

> **Never sacrifice Product for World.**

> **If forced to choose, Product wins.**

| Situation | Decision |
|---|---|
| Beautiful dome animation delays cascade legibility | **Product wins** — cut or simplify animation |
| Accurate brass shader hurts mobile performance → frame drops | **Product wins** — flat glow, keep timing |
| Minimal UI improves clarity but looks less "observatory" | **Product wins** |
| Ambient sound masks activation cues | **Product wins** — mix sound down |
| World detail creates onboarding friction | **Product wins** — remove detail |

World is **always optional enhancement** on a **non-optional** strategy core.

---

## How to tag work (required in hypotheses & reviews)

When proposing any change (evidence-driven or visual):

```
Bucket: PRODUCT | WORLD | BOTH

Product impact:
World impact:

If BOTH: which fails first if we cut scope?
Primary bucket (choose one): PRODUCT | WORLD
```

If **Primary = WORLD** and Product impact is neutral or negative → **do not ship** until Product impact is neutral or positive.

If **Primary = PRODUCT** → ship when evidence supports; World can follow in separate cycle (**one variable at a time**).
