## 2. Twenty minutes — reference game comparison

**Question:** Would a new player understand **strategy** after twenty minutes?

**Honest answer across aspirational games: No. They continue anyway.**

| Game | ~20 min typical state | Strategy understood? | Why they continue |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Chess** | Knows legal moves, maybe check/checkmate concept | **No** — openings, tactics, positional strategy opaque | First check, first capture, puzzle of "what's possible" |
| **Go** | Knows placement and capture rule | **No** — life/death, territory, shape strategy opaque | Board **feels** deep; each stone is an experiment |
| **Balatro** | Knows hand scoring basics | **No** — joker synergies, economy, scaling strategy opaque | Runs produce **surprising scores**; "one more run" |
| **Into the Breach** | Knows push/move/combat loop | **No** — perfect solutions, threat order strategy opaque | **Perfect information** makes each failure **learnable** |

**Pattern:** Twenty minutes buys **local competence + glimpse of depth**. Not **strategy**.

Strategy emerges over **hours to weeks** — through repetition, failure, and pattern accumulation.

**GLYPH is not underperforming reference games at this horizon.** It is matching them.

## 3. What level of uncertainty is desirable?

### Framework: three uncertainty layers

| Layer | Horizon | Desirable state at ~20 min | CEO (P1) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Operational** | Minute 1–5 | Low — "how do I act?" | ✅ Low |
| **Local causal** | Minute 5–20 | Medium — "why did that happen?" | ✅ Medium → resolving |
| **Strategic** | Hour 1+ | **High** — "what's the grand plan?" | ✅ High (expected) |

**Desirable at twenty minutes:**

- **Low** operational uncertainty  
- **Medium-to-low** local causal uncertainty (board mirror helps)  
- **High** strategic uncertainty — **on purpose**

**Undesirable at twenty minutes:**

- High operational uncertainty (*"how do I play?"*)  
- High **normative** uncertainty (*"am I allowed to / supposed to?"*)  
- Strategic uncertainty **without** curiosity (*"this is random, I'm done"*)

CEO has **high strategic uncertainty + high curiosity**. That is **desirable**.

## 4. Is "feels random" a problem signal?

**Not necessarily — at Performance 1.**

| "Random" means | Problem? |
|---|---|
| "Rules are hidden / dishonest" | **Yes** — §1 violation |
| "Outcomes feel arbitrary" | **Yes** — if local causality broken |
| "I don't yet see the strategic structure" | **No** — depth not yet exposed |
| "Too many variables I haven't mapped" | **No** — at 20 min with 1 performance |

CEO also **traced causality** (lit/cold, 7/8). That is incompatible with true "random" distrust. His "random" likely means **strategic opacity** — which is **healthy depth** until Performances 2+ or hours of play reveal structure.

**Do not conflate:** *strategic fog* with *broken trust*.
