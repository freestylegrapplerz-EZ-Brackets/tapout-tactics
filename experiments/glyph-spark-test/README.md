# GLYPH — Spark Experiments

Disprove-it experiments. Not the game. Same mechanics until Hope Pass proves otherwise.

## Milestone 1: Hope Pass (current)

**File:** `hope-pass.html` — open in any browser.

**Goal:** Increase hope without changing mechanics.

**Constitutional focus:** §2 Make players hope · §4 Frontier is lead actor · §9 Curtain call before credits · §1 Never lie

**What changed from v1 (performance only):**
- Score hidden during cascade; **credits after curtain call**
- **Frontier HUD** — chain count is the hero, not score
- **Travel lines** — energy visibly moves from cell to cell (natural approach time)
- **Lit vs cold** — board tells the story after the wave (evidence)
- **Accelerating rhythm** — steps compress as chain grows
- **Honest fuse** — 70ms beat on Action, not fake uncertainty

**What did NOT change:** rules, runes, board size, hand size, simulation, targets.

## Hope Pass — success criteria

Watch for (do not prompt):

- Player tracks the **wave**, not the score box
- Silent or vocal: *"come on…" "one more…" "YES!"*
- Payoff feels bigger than planning
- With credits hidden mid-run, player still cares

## Hope Pass — failure criteria

- "Neat" / "I get it" / one run and done
- Player asks "what's my score?" during cascade
- Still feels like numbers updating, not a performance

## Observation script

Say only: *"Place runes, click one to spark. Try to connect as many as you can."* Then stay quiet.

After: *"What were you watching?"* — listen for frontier/hope language vs score language.
